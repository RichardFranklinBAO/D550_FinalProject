Introduction

We analyze the Kaggle dataset S&P 500 stock data (camnugent/sandp500), which ~5 years of daily open, high, low, and close prices and trading volume (OHLCV) in all_stocks_5yr.csv. Our objectives are to:
1: programmatically obtain the data,
2: clean and aggregate it to monthly log returns,
3: construct a 121 cross-sectional momentum signal,
4: form decile portfolios and report the longshort (Q10Q1) performanceannualized return, volatility, Sharpe,
5: visualize gross vs.cost-adjusted cumulative returns.
A brief table summarizes sample coverage.

Table description:

The table summarizes the sample used in the 121 cross-sectional momentum test. We work from March 2014 to February 2018, covering 505 unique S&P-500 tickers and 29,507 stockmonths after aggregating daily prices to monthly log returns (last trading day of each month). The equal-weight longshort portfolio (long top decile by momentum, short bottom decile) delivers an average monthly log return of 0.45%, which annualizes to 4.05% with 17.38% annualized volatility, yielding a Sharpe ratio of 0.23. These are gross results (no transaction costs) and reflect a short sample and a simple specificationno industry/size neutrality or turnover controlso they should be viewed as a reproducible baseline rather than an optimized strategy.

The figure plots the cumulative wealth of the monthly longshort momentum portfolio starting at $1. This pattern is consistent with momentum cycles over the period and highlights that costs matter but are not dominant with the simple settings used here.
